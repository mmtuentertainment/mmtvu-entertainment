Questions

What owners ask before saying yes.

"We already have office staff for this."
That's exactly who this helps. The goal isn't replacing your staff — it's making sure routine follow-up happens every single time, even when your team is busy with other calls. Your staff still handles every real conversation. The system just makes sure no lead slips through the cracks and gives them a daily list of who to call first.
"We already use a CRM."
Good. For the first version, we don't need to replace it. We can start with one lead source — like forwarded quote-request emails or your website form — and run the follow-up system alongside your existing process. If it works, we can talk about deeper integration later.
"AI sounds risky for customer communication."
Agreed — that's why you approve every script before it goes live. Sensitive cases (angry customers, emergencies, warranty disputes, legal threats) are flagged for human handling immediately. The system identifies itself as your business, not as a fake human. And every message has an audit log.
"Is this going to be complicated to set up?"
No. We use tools you already know — Gmail, Google Sheets, Make.com or Zapier. Your team doesn't need to learn new software. We handle the entire setup. Your only job is to review scripts, test the daily summary, and tell us what to adjust.
"What if it doesn't work for my business?"
Cancel anytime — no contracts, no termination fees. The setup fee covers the work of building your system, but the monthly service is month-to-month. If you're not seeing value, stop paying. We track everything from day one so we can show you exactly what's happening with your leads.
"Do you replace our answering service?"
No. This is a follow-up layer, not an answering service. If you already use one, great — they capture the lead, we chase it. If a call goes to voicemail, the follow-up system still sends an SMS and email to that person within minutes. It works alongside whatever you already have.
"What about SMS compliance?"
We use SMS only when consent is explicit — either the lead provided their phone number in a service request context, or your intake form has proper opt-in language. Every SMS path supports STOP/HELP handling. We follow A2P 10DLC requirements if we use Twilio. Email is used first when consent is uncertain. You own legal approval for all customer communications.
