## Problem Statement

MMTVUEntertainment should become a parent company that owns multiple income streams. The first completed stream is AI Tool Hub, an affiliate/media asset under the MMTVUEntertainment umbrella. The main business now needs an active cashflow engine that can make money faster than affiliate SEO.

The recommended first active engine is a productized AI automation service for home service businesses with office staff. The wedge is inconsistent and expensive follow-up: leads, quotes, reminders, and customer reactivation that humans handle manually or inconsistently.

## Demand Evidence

Direct demand evidence is not proven yet. That is the biggest risk.

Current evidence is market-logic evidence:

- Home service businesses already spend money to acquire leads through Google Ads, Angi, Thumbtack, referrals, and local SEO.
- A single booked job can be worth hundreds to thousands of dollars.
- Many shops already pay office staff or answering services, so the budget category exists.
- Slow or inconsistent follow-up directly loses revenue.
- AI automation has a clear ROI story when tied to booked estimates, quote follow-up, no-show reduction, and reactivation.

What does not count yet:

- No customer has paid MMTVUEntertainment for this.
- No specific home service owner has validated the offer.
- No pilot has been sold.
- No before/after revenue result exists.

The first assignment is therefore not “build a platform.” The first assignment is to test whether owners will pay for the outcome.

## Status Quo

The target business already has office staff, but the follow-up process is inconsistent and expensive.

Typical workflow today:

1. Lead comes in by phone, website form, Google Business Profile, Facebook, Angi, Thumbtack, or referral.
2. Office staff manually responds if they have time.
3. Some leads get one callback or email.
4. Quote follow-up is inconsistent.
5. Old estimates and past customers are rarely reactivated.
6. The owner has limited visibility into whether staff followed up properly.
7. Staff time is spent on repetitive tasks instead of high-value customer conversations.

This creates a strong wedge: do not replace the office. Add an AI follow-up layer that makes the office more consistent and measurable.

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge

First customer type:

- Home service companies: plumbers, HVAC, roofers, electricians, and similar local service operators.
- Best initial sub-niche: HVAC and plumbing, because job value is high and speed-to-lead matters.
- Buyer: owner/operator or general manager.
- Internal user: office manager / dispatcher / customer service rep.

The narrowest wedge:

“AI follow-up layer for home service businesses with office staff.”

Smallest paid version:

- Captures new quote requests from form/email/manual entry.
- Sends instant SMS/email follow-up.
- Asks basic qualifying questions.
- Nudges unbooked leads for 7 days.
- Reminds quoted prospects after estimates.
- Sends owner/staff a daily summary of hot leads and stale leads.

Do not start with a full CRM. Do not start with a giant AI platform. Start with follow-up that produces booked appointments.

## Premises

1. MMTVUEntertainment should be the parent company, not the first product.
2. AI Tool Hub should sit under MMTVUEntertainment as income stream #1: media/affiliate asset.
3. The fastest path to revenue is a productized AI service, not waiting for SEO affiliate traffic.
4. The first customer niche should be home services.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Productized AI Follow-Up Service

Summary: Sell a done-for-you AI follow-up system to home service businesses. Use existing tools, simple automation, SMS/email workflows, and manual setup. Charge setup plus monthly retainer.

Effort: Medium
Risk: Medium

Pros:

- Fastest path to cash.
- Does not require building a SaaS platform first.
- Easy ROI story: more booked estimates, fewer stale leads, less staff time wasted.
- Can be sold locally or by cold outreach.
- Each setup teaches the future product.

Cons:

- Requires sales calls and owner conversations.
- Early fulfillment may be manual and messy.
- Needs careful messaging so staff do not feel threatened.
- Compliance around SMS/email opt-in must be handled.

Reuses:

- AI Tool Hub as proof of AI/content capability.
- Existing Hermes automation ability.
- Future affiliate links for tools used in client stacks.

### Approach B: Build a SaaS Platform First

Summary: Build a web app for home service AI follow-up before selling. Businesses sign up, connect forms/CRM, and run automations themselves.

Effort: Large
Risk: High

Pros:

- More scalable if demand is proven.
- Cleaner long-term asset.
- Easier to demonstrate once built.

Cons:

- Slowest path to revenue.
- High chance of building the wrong thing.
- Requires integrations, auth, billing, deliverability, SMS compliance, onboarding, and support.
- No proof yet that anyone wants this exact product.

Reuses:

- Could eventually reuse learnings from service clients.

### Approach C: Media + Lead Gen First

Summary: Build more AI Tool Hub-style content and lead-gen pages for home service automation. Monetize via affiliate links, inbound leads, and future services.

Effort: Medium
Risk: Medium-High

Pros:

- Builds long-term asset under MMTVUEntertainment.
- Fits the existing AI Tool Hub content pipeline.
- Can create credibility and inbound demand over time.

Cons:

- SEO is slow.
- Does not validate willingness to pay quickly.
- Content without sales can become fake progress.
- Home service owners may not discover or trust generic content early.

Reuses:

- AI Tool Hub pipeline, GitHub Pages, cron publishing, affiliate system.

## Recommended Approach

Choose Approach A: Productized AI Follow-Up Service.

Reason: MMTVUEntertainment needs cashflow proof before building more assets. Approach A creates direct customer contact, direct revenue potential, and a learning loop. If it works, the workflow can later become a SaaS product, a template pack, a content engine, and an affiliate channel.

## The Assignment

Within 48 hours, contact 10 home service businesses and ask one question:

“When a quote request comes in, how many times does your team follow up before giving up?”

Do not pitch first. Get the real workflow. If they complain about inconsistent follow-up, slow staff, missed quotes, or no-show leads, then ask:

“If I could set up an automated follow-up system that chased those leads for you and gave your office staff a daily hot-lead list, would that be worth testing?”

The goal is not to close all 10. The goal is to hear the same pain in the customer’s own words.
